Understanding the Different Types of Car Glass Damage

Your vehicle's glass serves more than just an aesthetic purpose; it plays a vital role in your safety and driving experience. Car glass damage is a common issue that can compromise both your vehicle's structural integrity and your ability to navigate the road safely. In this article, we'll delve into the various types of car glass damage and how Auto Glass Chandler can help with auto glass repair and windshield repair in Chandler.

Cracks

Cracks are one of the most prevalent forms of car glass damage. They can vary in size, shape, and location on your windshield or windows. Cracks can be caused by a variety of factors, including rocks, debris, or temperature fluctuations. Even small cracks can compromise the integrity of your glass and obstruct your view of the road.

Chips are typically the result of small rocks or other debris impacting your car's glass. While they may seem minor, they can quickly worsen if left unattended. Chips not only compromise the structural integrity of the glass but can also obstruct your vision.

Bull's-Eye Damage

A bull's-eye is a type of circular damage on the glass that resembles a target. It's usually caused by a concentrated impact, often from a larger object like a stone. Bull's-eye damage is characterized by a central point of impact with radiating cracks.

 Star Break

Star breaks are similar to bull's-eye damage but have multiple short radial cracks extending outward from a central point. These are typically the result of significant impact and can vary in size and complexity.

Combination Break

A combination break is a more complex form of damage that combines elements of bull's-eye, star break, and cracks. These can be challenging to repair and often require windshield replacement.
